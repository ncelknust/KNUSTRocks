Built on real national data

The Enhanced Rock Weathering Suitability Index combines three equally auditable drivers, each rescaled to 0–1 by continuous membership functions and summarised to every district, then combined into a single 0–100 score.

  • Soil (S): pH, CEC and organic carbon — SoilGrids, 250 m
  • Climate (C): rainfall and temperature — WorldClim, ~1 km
  • Geology (G): reactive feedstock — Geological Survey of Ghana 1:1,000,000
  • Robustness tested across four weighting scenarios (Spearman ρ = 0.88–0.99)
The index
ERW-SI = 100 · (0.40 S + 0.30 C + 0.30 G)

Ghana ERW Suitability — Technical Siting Tool

Where in Ghana is enhanced rock weathering most suitable, and why?

This map scores all 216 districts on a 0–100 ERW Suitability Index (ERW-SI), built from three equally auditable drivers on real national data (Geological Survey of Ghana geology, SoilGrids soils, WorldClim climate):

Soil (S)
Restoration need — acidic, low-fertility soils gain most from ERW.
Climate (C)
Rainfall & heat that drive feedstock dissolution.
Geology (G)
Availability of reactive silicate feedstock.

ERW-SI = 40%·S + 30%·C + 30%·G by default — you can re-weight it live.
